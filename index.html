Chapter 1 (Leaving Home)

    Kate stared intensively out of the window of the fast moving bus which strode through the rough roads of woodworth villa. She felt a sharp pain strike through her heart as she watched the orange tatched-roofed houses fade, she knew she was going to miss woodsworth and all it's magnificent features,
"besides, just a year away wouldnt hurt and it would look like I never left"  she remminded herself gracefully while shutting her beautiful sea blue eyes to take a nap, but seemed impossible as the thought of a new life in greenwhich with Mrs. Mabel kept hunting her with great excitement mixed with severe anxiousness.
   It was her first real job ever since she resigned as a bar attendant at the prestigious 'de luna' hotel some years back. And now, she will be a care giver to Old Mabel, who was on a vacation in the north province of Greenwhich city.
She had repeated tried to imagine how green which would turn out to be, could it be as peaceful and serene as woodsworth or just one of those noisy cities with crowded areas and a polluted atmosphere which she just couldnt bare. "it should be at least, conducive enough to get a good afternoon sleep"-- she wished knowing how much she loved sleeping into the afternoons.

   "I think this dropped from you" a deep voice echoed gently from beside her, slashing through her wavy thoughts. "Jeez"-- she was startled, she hadn't quite noticed the figure sitted next to her. He was dark-skinned in faded levis and open-necked check shirt, sleeves rolled up to the elbows, sunlight glinting on the dark chest-hair and strong, tanned arms, holding out to her, what looks a fragile clothing material. she was uncertain about what it actually was, not until she learnt forward to take a closer look.
"Holy shit"-- it came out unconciously as she reached out with what seemed faster than the speed of light to grab the material while forcefully squeezing it into one of the bags she had carefully placed on her thighs. she could see the look he had on his face and it sure wasn't helping matters. only if the earth could open up and swallow her right now, it would save her from the feeling of the gravious embarrassment running through her body.
She swore she could have cried if she was still the 15 year old that she was 12 years ago.
